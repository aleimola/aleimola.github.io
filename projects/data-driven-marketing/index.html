Our Company

CONTEXT: This project examines the introduction of Neovision, an innovative model of cinematic consumption developed in response to the progressive decline of traditional cinema, a phenomenon intensified by the COVID-19 pandemic and the rapid expansion of streaming platforms. Within this context, the project is grounded in the assumption that audiences have not lost interest in cinema itself, but rather increasingly demand immersive, personalized, and emotionally engaging experiences capable of motivating them to move beyond home-based viewing.

WHO WE ARE: Neovision proposes an experiential cinema service that integrates technological innovation with cultural heritage enhancement. Film screenings take place in historic and artistic locations, specifically prepared for the event, and are experienced through next-generation smart glasses equipped with integrated audiovisual systems and interactive content. Screenings are often complemented by ancillary activities—such as meetings with actors, directors, experts, live performances, and educational formats—which further enhance the symbolic, cultural, and emotional value of the experience.

SERVICE: The service offering is structured into several pricing and access models (single tickets, premium subscriptions, themed cards, and tailored packages for schools and families), with the aim of addressing heterogeneous demand segments characterized by different cultural preferences and willingness to pay. At the same time, Neovision maintains a traditional cinema service component, a strategic choice that allows the company to mitigate entrepreneurial risk and ensure economic sustainability in the medium term.

MISSION

Neovision’s mission is to deliver a new form of cinematic experience that combines technological innovation, cultural heritage, and sustainable entertainment. The objective is to move beyond the conventional cinema setting, transforming film viewing into an immersive, inclusive, and accessible event capable of fostering a deep connection between the spectator, the cinematic narrative, and the surrounding historical and artistic environment.

VISION

Neovision’s vision is to become a benchmark for the cinema of the future, where technology, culture, and art converge to create multisensory and shared experiences. In this perspective, cinema is no longer conceived solely as audiovisual content, but as a comprehensive experience involving places, emotions, and social interaction, while preserving the central value of collective viewing.

BRAND VALUES
  • Innovation: Conscious use of technology to enhance—rather than replace—the cinematic experience.
  • Cultural Heritage: Sustainable use of historic locations as an integral part of the narrative.
  • Sustainability: Promoting an entertainment model that respects both places and local communities.
  • Spectator Centrality: Audience as an active participant in an immersive and emotional journey.
  • Quality & Emotion: Defining elements designed to create meaningful and memorable experiences.
MARKET POSITIONING

Neovision is strategically positioned as an experiential and immersive cinema service, located at the intersection of technological innovation, cultural heritage enhancement, and high emotional involvement. Unlike traditional cinemas and streaming platforms, Neovision does not compete primarily on price or volume, but on the quality and intensity of the experience offered.

Across the positioning maps, Neovision consistently scores high in:

  • Experience, through the use of smart glasses and interactive audiovisual content;
  • Cultural heritage valorization, by integrating historic locations into the cinematic narrative;
  • Emotional involvement, generated by immersive settings, live performances, and curated events;
  • Customer care, reflecting the attention to comfort, service quality, and overall experience.

These dimensions allow Neovision to clearly differentiate itself from both direct competitors (such as open-air cinemas and experiential film events) and indirect competitors (including multiplex cinemas, immersive museums, and streaming platforms). The resulting positioning supports a premium and experience-oriented value proposition, targeted at an audience seeking cultural enrichment, emotional intensity, and innovative forms of entertainment.

Analysis RFM e K-Means

This section presents a concise overview of the analytical framework adopted to examine customer behavior. The dashboard summarizes the main methodological steps and results of the RFM analysis and clustering procedure, highlighting the criteria used for data construction, scoring, and segmentation. The objective is to provide a clear and structured synthesis of the underlying analytical process, while detailed assumptions, formulas, and graphical outputs are documented separately.

RFM Analysis & Clustering

The analysis is based on the application of the RFM model (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) over an 18-month time horizon (01/01/2024 – 30/06/2025), considered appropriate for capturing stable consumption behaviors, seasonal effects, and post-launch dynamics of the service. Two distinct populations were analyzed: private customers and family customers, treated separately in order to avoid distortions arising from heterogeneous consumption patterns.

For both populations, the following three key dimensions were considered:

  • Recency (R): number of days elapsed since the last purchase;
  • Frequency (F): number of events attended;
  • Monetary (M): total expenditure.

R, F, and M scores were assigned on a 1–5 scale, using the distribution quantiles, and subsequently aggregated into an overall RFM score.

Data Generation

The data used in the analysis consist of simulated data, constructed on the basis of assumptions consistent with the business model described in the project.

Frequency and Monetary
In order to ensure a realistic relationship between consumption frequency and expenditure:

  • a latent variable, Reference, was introduced and generated as a uniform random variable in the interval [0,1];
  • Frequency and Monetary were computed as linear functions of predefined value ranges;
  • a noise term (Storm) was added to prevent perfect correlation between the two variables.

Recency
Last purchase dates were generated randomly within the observation time window. The Recency variable was calculated as the difference, expressed in days, between the snapshot date (30/06/2025) and the date of the last transaction.

K-means Clustering

A K-means clustering procedure was applied to the RFM_points obtained from the RFM analysis, with the objective of segmenting customers into homogeneous groups based on observed purchasing behaviors.

The optimal number of clusters was determined using the Elbow Method, by analyzing the trend of the Total Within Sum of Squares (TWSS) as the number of clusters k increases. In both analyzed cases (private customers and families), the graph shows a marked reduction in TWSS up to k = 4, beyond which further improvements become marginal. Therefore, k = 4 was selected as the optimal number of clusters, as it provides a balance between the descriptive power of the model and the stability of the segmentation.

The analysis of variable contributions to the clustering dimensions reveals a clear interpretative structure. In particular, Frequency (F_points) and Monetary (M_points) provide contributions significantly above the average to the first dimension, emerging as the dominant variables defining this axis. This result is consistent with the data generation process, which introduces a controlled correlation between consumption frequency and total expenditure.

The second clustering dimension is instead predominantly explained by Recency (R_points), whose contribution is markedly higher than that of the other two variables. This evidence is attributable to the fact that Recency was constructed from the Last Purchase variable independently of Frequency and Monetary.

The resulting segmentation therefore makes it possible to distinguish groups of customers characterized by different combinations of:

  • economic value (Monetary),
  • intensity of service usage (Frequency),
  • recent activity level (Recency),

thus providing a coherent quantitative basis for the analysis of behavioral profiles.

Output of the Analysis

The integration of RFM scoring and K-means clustering produces a segmentation of the customer base based exclusively on observable behavioral variables (Recency, Frequency, Monetary).

The outcome of the analysis consists of:

  • a partition of customers into disjoint clusters, obtained by minimizing intra-cluster variability and maximizing inter-cluster separation;
  • the identification of statistically homogeneous groups in terms of relative levels of Recency, Frequency, and Monetary;
  • a synthetic representation of consumption behavior distributions, useful for comparisons between populations (private customers vs families) and for subsequent analyses based on RFM scores.

Topic Analysis-TALL

Manual Topic Analysis

The qualitative topic analysis conducted on interviews and focus groups led to the identification of six key topics describing users’ perceptions of the Neovision experiential cinema service:

COODING TABLE
Attraction and Curiosity

Users express strong interest and curiosity toward the novelty of immersive cinema, perceiving the experience as innovative and emotionally engaging compared to traditional movie-going.

Immersive and Social Experience

The smart glass technology enhances immersion, while users simultaneously value the possibility of sharing the experience with family, friends, and educational groups.

Comfort, Safety, and Usability

Concerns emerge regarding prolonged smart glass use, particularly in terms of physical comfort, potential health effects, and hygiene of shared devices.

Price and Economic Risk

Users show high price sensitivity, especially families, questioning whether the experiential cinema justifies a higher cost than traditional alternatives.

Trust and Risk Reduction

Safeguard mechanisms, such as subscription conversion or refund options, increase user confidence and reduce perceived financial risk.

Cultural and Educational Value

Neovision is perceived as offering added cultural and educational value through curated content, post-screening events, and the use of historical locations.

TALL Topic Analysis

Objective of the Analysis

To apply an automated textual analysis (TALL software) in order to validate and strengthen the results of the manual qualitative analysis, enhancing interpretative transparency and methodological robustness.

Dataset

  • 20 textual documents, aggregated by interview question
  • Responses collected through individual interviews and focus groups
  • Pre-processing included targeted Part-of-Speech selection (adjectives, nouns, verbs, multiwords) and lexical filtering to improve semantic relevance

Key Findings

Lexical Analysis (WordCloud & Co-word Analysis)

  • Core concept: experience, recurrent across the entire corpus
  • Strong emphasis on the immersive and emotional dimension (cinema, event, film, feel)
  • Clear emergence of curiosity and openness to innovation, especially in cultural contexts
  • Identification of perceived critical issues related to comfort, physical aspects, and technological concerns

Specific semantic clusters:

  • Students and learning → educational and didactic value of the experience
  • Technology and comfort → balance between innovation, cost, and usability

Topic Analysis (4 Macro-Themes)

1. Service Structure and User Concerns
Technical evaluation of the experience (comfort, technology, usability), with attention to potential limitations.

2. Cultural and Social Value
The experience is perceived as a participatory and culturally enriching event, strongly linked to the venue and its context.

3. Curiosity and Immersive Innovation
Attraction toward smart glasses and interest in technological novelty and immersive potential.

4. Student Target and Economic Risk Management
Focus on schools and students, with perceived risk linked to deviation from traditional cinema formats.

Topic Correlations

  • Curiosity ↔ comfort concerns: strong interest coexists with practical worries
  • Cultural value ↔ immersion: the event-based context amplifies emotional engagement
  • Risk ↔ reduced enthusiasm: perceived technological issues dampen attraction
  • Near-independence between technical aspects and cultural value, which evolve in parallel

Sentiment Analysis

  • 85% of documents show positive or very positive sentiment
  • 15% of documents show negative or very negative sentiment

Drivers of Positive Sentiment

  • Innovation and initial curiosity
  • Cultural prestige of the locations
  • Reduction of perceived economic risk
  • Strong educational value (school segment)
  • Improved family and social experience

Drivers of Negative Sentiment

  • Physical discomfort (nausea, weight, visual fatigue)
  • Concerns regarding hygiene and technological reliability
  • General criticism of contemporary cinema (not of the service itself)

Operational Strategies for Clusters (Private Customers)

From the RFM Analysis combined with K-Means Clustering, it was possible to identify four customer segments. For each of these segments, we have designed targeted operational strategies:

VIP & Champions

Objective: Loyalty & Advocacy

  • “Behind the Scenes” Access: Exclusive invitations to private meetings with guests and behind-the-scenes activities.
  • Elite Seat Offering: Priority access to the most comfortable and desirable locations in the venue.
  • Referral Program: "Bring a friend" promotions with exclusive discounts on gadgets or aperitifs.
New Customers

Objective: Conversion to VIP

  • Fast Upgrade Strategy: Email invitation for Life Moment Card with previous ticket cost deduction (48h window).
  • Welcome Gift: Branded gadgets or film-related items to enhance the first-time satisfaction.
  • Referral Program: Referral discounts primarily focused on fostering a shared social experience.
At-Risk Customers

Objective: Re-engagement & Recovery

  • Suggestion form: Using short email questionnaires to identify precise reasons for disengagement.
  • Social Reconnection: Free invitations to exclusive events to restore the emotional brand connection.
  • “A Sneak Peek”: Exclusive low-commitment subscriptions designed to reduce pressure during return.
Hibernating Customers

Objective: Final Reactivation Attempt

  • Targeted Reactivation: Simple emails showcasing the renewed service to spark new curiosity.
  • Re-permission Email: Professional final contact to offer a choice before discontinuing communication.

Operational Strategies for Clusters (Families)

From the RFM analysis combined with K-means clustering, it was possible to identify four family segments. For each of these segments, we have designed targeted operational strategies:

VIP & Champions

Objective: Exclusivity & Community Status

  • Reserved Parking: Facilitated access to dedicated parking areas to enhance convenience.
  • Theatrical Entrance: Welcomed through an exclusive entrance with character-themed reception.
  • Legacy Album: A technology-enhanced memory diary that evolves with the family’s Neovision journey.
New Families

Objective: Establish Family Tradition

  • Welcome Area: A game-like tutorial led by entertainers to introduce the Neovision experience.
  • Movie Gadgets: Free themed items provided to create an immediate positive association.
  • Themed Events: Example: Moana-themed treasure hunts held immediately after screenings to celebrate anniversaries.
  • time limited discount: Time-limited discounts on uncompleted sections to encourage immediate return.
At-Risk Families

Objective: Rekindle Interest

  • time limited discount: Time-limited discounts on uncompleted sections to encourage immediate return.
  • Combined Tactics: All strategies defined for individual at-risk customers also apply here.
Hibernating Families

Objective: The “Great Awakening”

  • Loss Aversion Credit: A €15 account credit valid for 10 days to leverage the tendency to avoid losing value.
  • “Dusty Passport”: Re-engagement emails highlighting their long absence with a personal invite to upcoming films.
School Package

Neovision also introduced a dedicated package for schools, screening films with strong historical, educational, and ethical value. This includes both upcoming releases and past cinema classics, which can also be curated directly at the schools' request.

Strategic Insights based on Survey Crosstabs

Age vs. Preferred Location

15-24 Age Group: Significant preference for natural settings (beaches) or panoramic rooftops. Neovision plans to combine these screenings with DJ sets, aperitifs, and parties.

Over 25 Target: Strong preference for historic villas, castles, and botanical gardens. Offerings will focus on smart glasses screenings of iconic, timeless films.

Genre vs. Type of Location
  • Horror & Castles: Hosted in dark, historic settings combined with cultural talks by genre experts.
  • Classics & Historic Villas: Example: "La Dolce Vita" at Casina Valadier. The architectural style perfectly complements the film's atmosphere.
  • Action & Rooftops: Elevated settings enhance themes of adventure, danger, and intense excitement.
Price vs. Additional Services

€20–30 Price Association: Young people (15-24) are primarily interested in screenings with smart glasses only.

  • Periodic Offers: Monthly promotions or "Black Friday" events to meet budget constraints.
  • Special Discounts: Holiday-themed offers (Christmas, Halloween, Ferragosto) for price-sensitive targets.

Premium Experience: Customers with higher willingness to pay show strong interest in additional activities, such as guided location visits.

Age vs. Preferred Companionship

The analysis indicates that individuals aged 15 to 24 overwhelmingly prefer to experience Neovision together with friends. To capitalize on this social preference, Neovision has planned specialized group offers and shared experiences.
